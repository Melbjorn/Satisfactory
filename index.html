Introduction

Satisfactory is a new game from Coffee Stain Studios, the creators of Goat Simulator and the Sanctum series. Satisfactory is a game of factory management and planet exploitation. As a pioneer working for FICSIT Inc., you are dropped onto an alien planet with a handful of tools and must harvest the planet's natural resources to construct increasingly complex factories for automating all your resources needs.

After you are set up, it will be time to build the Space Elevator and begin Project Assembly, supplying FICSIT with increasingly numerous and complex components for their unknown purposes.

The main focus of the game lies in constructing buildings and linking them together with Conveyor Belts so that your factory can handle the entire process of item construction for you, including mining, transport, delivery, and generating power to keep itself running.

Start Up

As you start playing the game, you are given the task of constructing the HUB as a way of doing a tutorial for the gameplay.

You start by needing to gather all the resources by hand, with a pick that is provided to you by FICSIT Inc., but soon you are given recipes for constructing "Buildings" that gather the resources for you.

After the HUB is fully constructed you will be able to build an Miner Mk.1, Smelter, and Constructor to make items for you.

Automation

The main purpose of the game is to automate the creation process which falls into 3 main categories.

Resource Gathering:

This is the obtaining of the raw material needed to get refined for use in the game. Some things like water and oil can't be gotten manually.

  • Portable Miner
    2 Iron Plates + 4 Iron Rods

  • Miner Mk.1
    1 Portable Miner + 10 Iron Plates + 10 Concrete

  • Miner Mk.2
    2 Portable Miners + 10 Encased Industrial Beams + 20 Steel Pipes + 10 Modular Frames

  • Miner Mk.3
    3 Portable Miners + 50 Steel Pipes + 5 Supercomputers + 10 Fused Modular Frames + 3 Turbo Motors

  • Water Extractor
    20 Copper Sheets + 10 Reinforced Iron Plates + 10 Rotors

  • Oil Extractor
    15 Motors + 20 Encased Industrial Beams + 60 Cables

  • Resource Well Pressurizer
    200 Wire + 50 Rubber + 50 Encased Industrial Beams + 50 Motors

  • Resource Well Extractor
    10 Steel Beams + 10 Plastic

Resource Refining:

This takes the raw material and refines it into a useable form to create items in the game.

  • Smelter
    5 Iron Rods + 8 Wire

  • Foundry
    10 Modular Frames + 10 Rotors + 20 Concrete

  • Refinery
    10 Motors + 10 Encased Industrial Beams + 30 Steel Pipes + 20 Copper Sheets

Item Creation:

This is final and most elaborate stage where refined materials make items and certain items get used to make other items.

  • Constructor
    2 Reinforced Iron Plates + 8 Cables

  • Assembler
    8 Reinforced Iron Plates + 4 Rotors + 10 Cables

  • Manufacturer
    5 Motors + 10 Heavy Modular Frames + 50 Cables + 50 Plastic

  • Packager
    20 Steel Beams + 10 Rubber + 10 Plastic

  • Blender
    20 Motors + 10 Heavy Modular Frames + 50 Aluminum Casings + 5 Radio Control Units

  • Particle Accelerator
    25 Radio Control Units + 100 Electromagnetic Control Rods + 10 Supercomputers + 50 Cooling Systems + 20 Fused Modular Frames + 10 Turbo Motors

Automated Power

Every building for creating items uses power so it is a good idea to also have a way to create power automatically.

One of the ways to create power require you to manually gather recourses that can be burned for fuel.

  • Biomass Burner
    15 Iron Plates + 15 Iron Rods + 25 Wire

All other ways of generating power are able to be setup to receive the needed materials automatically.

  • Coal Generator
    20 Reinforced Iron Plates + 10 Rotors + 30 Cables

  • Fuel Generator
    5 Computers + 10 Heavy Modular Frames + 15 Motors + 50 Plastic + 50 Quickwire

  • Nuclear Power Plant
    250 Concrete + 25 Heavy Modular Frames + 5 Supercomputers + 100 Cables + 100 Alclad Aluminum Sheets

There is also a way to store power that can be used later in cases of high consumption.

  • Power Storage
    100 Wire + 10 Modular Frames + 5 Stators

Storage

It is important to have places to keep things as they are built so you can use them throughout the game.

There are 3 different item storage boxes that can be unlocked in the game.

  • Storage Box
    6 Iron Plates + 6 Iron Rods

  • Storage Container
    10 Iron Plates + 10 Iron Rods

  • Industrial Storage
    20 Steel Beams + 20 Steel Pipes

There are 2 personalized storage boxes you can purchase with in game credits called FICSIT coupons.

  • Medical Storage Box
    6 Iron Plates + 6 Iron Rods

  • Hazard Storage Box
    6 Iron Plates + 6 Iron Rods

Lastly there are other containers that can store different resources.

  • Fluid Buffer
    10 Copper Sheets + 5 Modular Frames

  • Industrial Fluid Buffer
    30 Plastic + 3 Heavy Modular Frames

  • Power Storage
    100 Wire + 10 Modular Frames + 5 Stators

Factories

Factories are considered the way to achieve the goal of the game. They are used to make the items. Sending items to FICSIT via the HUB unlocks new buildings and new items to make, which progresses the game.

You start with simple factories to make the first main items.

  • Concrete
    Miner Mk.1(on a normal Limestone node set to 150%) + 2 Constructors = 30 Concrete per min.

  • Iron Pipes
    Miner Mk.1(on a normal Iron node) + 2 Smelters + 4 Constructors = 60 Iron Pipes per min.

  • Iron Plates
    Miner Mk.1(on a normal Iron node) + 2 Smelters + 2 Constructors = 40 Iron Plates per min.

  • Wire
    Miner Mk.1(on a normal Copper node) + 2 Smelters + 4 Constructors = 120 Wire per min.

  • Cables
    1 Wire factory + 2 Constructors = 60 Cables per min.

These factories get used over and over to make more elaborate items. You see this initially when making Cables. You first have to make a Wire factory then use that to make Cables. You could use the same original Wire factory and only turn half of it into a Cables factory resulting in 60 Wire per min. and 30 Cables per min. with only 1 Miner Mk.1.

  • Truck Station

3 of the vehicles can be bought from the AWESOME Shop

  • FICSIT Factory Cart
    4 Reinforced Iron Plates + 4 Iron Rods + 2 Rotors

  • Golden Factory Cart

  • Cyber Wagon
    Purchased from the AWESOME Shop for 20 FICSIT coupons

2 are unlocked as you go through the stages of the HUB to unlock new items and buildings.

  • Tractor
    5 Modular Frames + 5 Rotors + 10 Reinforced Iron Plates

  • Truck
    15 Motors + 10 Circuit Boards + 5 Heavy Modular Frames + 50 Rubber + 20 Encased Industrial Beams

1 all-terrain vehicle is unlocked by completing the Quartz Research Chain in the MAM

  • Explorer
    5 Crystal Oscillators + 5 Motors + 15 Steel Pipes + 5 Heavy Modular Frames

Trains

Currently the fastest land vehicle in-game is the train. It has the ability to be setup to go to connected Train Stations and deliver both Solid and Fluid resources. Train Stations are connected by Railways and trains can only be built on Railways as well.

There are only 2 different train pieces. Empty Freight Cars can have either Solid or Fluid containers put on them.

  • Electric Locomotive
    5 Heavy Modular Frames + 10 Motors + 15 Steel Pipes + 5 Computers

  • Freight Car
    4 Heavy Modular Frames + 10 Steel Pipes

These are the pieces that make up a train track.

  • Railway
    1 Steel Pipe + 1 Steel Beam

  • Train Station
    4 Heavy Modular Frames + 8 Computers + 50 Concrete + 25 Cables

  • Freight Platform
    6 Heavy Modular Frames + 2 Computers + 50 Concrete + 25 Cables + 5 Motors

  • Fluid Freight Platform
    6 Heavy Modular Frames + 2 Computers + 50 Concretes + 25 Cables + 5 Motors

  • Empty Platform
    6 Heavy Modular Frames + 50 Concrete

  • Empty Platform With Catwalk
    6 Heavy Modular Frames + 50 Concrete

Other Transportation

There is 1 other way to transport items in the air using Aeronautical Engineering.

  • Drone
    4 Motors + 10 Alclad Aluminum Sheets + 1 Radio Control Unit + 2 AI Limiters + 1 Portable Miner

  • Drone Port
    20 Heavy Modular Frames + 20 High-Speed Connectors + 50 Alclad Aluminum Sheets + 50 Aluminum Casings + 10 Radio Control Units

There are 2 ways the Pioneer can travel around using external sources with the Jump Pad and Hypertube.

  • Jump Pad
    2 Rotors + 15 Iron Plates + 10 Cables

  • U-Jelly Landing Pad
    2 Rotors + 20 Cables + 200 Biomass

  • Hypertube
    1 Copper Sheet + 1 Steel Pipe

  • Hypertube Entrance
    4 Encased Industrial Beams + 4 Rotors + 10 Steel Pipes

Lastly, there are 3 ways that the Pioneer can move with equipment the have equipped. They can zip along powerlines if they have the Zipline in their hand or fly around if they are wearing a Jetpack or a Hover Pack.

  • Zipline
    1 Xeno-Zapper + 30 Quickwire + 3 Iron Rods + 10 Cables

  • Jetpack
    50 Plastic + 50 Rubber + 15 Circuit Boards + 5 Motors

  • Hover Pack
    8 Motors + 4 Heavy Modular Frames + 8 Computers + 40 Alclad Aluminum Sheets

Creatures

There are two different major types of creatures in the game, passive and hostile.

Passive:

  • Lizard Doggo
    Short reptilian dog-like creature, which can be tamed by the Pioneer.

  • Non Flying Bird
    Small bird-like creature that sticks to the undergrowth.

  • Power Slug
    Glowing immobile slugs that can found around the world.

  • Space Giraffe-Tick-Penguin-Whale Thing
    Passive land creature found throughout the world.

  • Giant Flying Manta
    Large flying creature that patrols around the world.

Hostile:

  • Flying Crab
    Small flying creature that continually attacks the Pioneer.

  • Fluffy-tailed Hog
    Melee creature that the Pioneer can encounter while exploring.

  • Alpha Hog
    Larger version of Fluffy-tailed Hog that is also considerably more dangerous.

  • Cliff Hog
    Melee, and rock throwing creature that the Pioneer can encounter while exploring.

  • Nuclear Hog
    Melee, rock throwing, radioactive creature that the Pioneer can encounter while exploring.

  • Spitter
    Class of ranged hostile creature found in the world.

  • Alpha Spitter
    Stronger and larger version of the Spitter.

  • Baby Stinger
    Melee spider-like creatures found mostly in caves and jungles.

  • Alpha Stinger
    Melee, and jumping spider-like creatures found mostly in caves and jungles.

  • Elite Gas Stinger
    Melee, jumping, gaseous spider-like creatures found mostly in caves and jungles.

Weapons

You start the game with a Xeno-Zapper equipped in your inventory for any hostile creatures you come across. However, if you die with it in your inventory you will have to track it down or make a new one.

  • Xeno-Zapper
    10 Iron Rods + 2 Reinforced Iron Plates + 15 Cables + 50 Wire

Soon you can unlock the direct upgrade by advancing the stages of the HUB and make the Xeno-Basher.

  • Xeno-Basher
    5 Modular Frames + 2 Xeno-Zappers + 25 Cables + 500 Wire

You can unlock 2 guns that require Ammo by completing the Alien Organisms Research Chain and the Sulfur Research Chain in the MAM.

  • Rebar Gun
    6 Reinforced Iron Plates + 16 Iron Rods + 100 Screws

  • Rifle
    2 Motors + 10 Rubber + 25 Steel Pipes + 250 Screws

Different Ammo can be unlocked with other Research Chains as well in the MAM. (Learn about them on the Official Satisfactory Wiki page listed below.)

Blueprints

Blueprints are created using a Blueprint Designer.

  • Blueprint Designer
    15 Modular Frames + 25 Cables + 100 Concrete + 100 Steel Beams

Blueprints can be used to create entire parts of a factory that fit within a 4 by 4 foundation area. Blueprints must first be made in the designer and saved then they can be accessed in the same menu as the rest of the buildings you can make. Recently it was added that you can dismantle an entire blueprint with a single action by changing the dismantle gun to blueprint mode.
